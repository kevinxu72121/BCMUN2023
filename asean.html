Association of Southeast Asian Nations
(ASEAN)

Member states in Southeast Asia make up the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), a regional intergovernmental organization. Since its founding in 1967, ASEAN has worked to advance regional integration and collaboration, boost social and economic advancement, and uphold regional peace and stability. You will get the chance to represent a member state in the ASEAN committee at the Model UN and participate in dynamic discussions and negotiations to solve issues pertaining to social justice, economic development, and regional security. The ASEAN committee offers delegates a distinctive and interesting opportunity to learn about the issues affecting Southeast Asia and hone their leadership, critical thinking, and public speaking abilities. These issues range from combating transnational crime to supporting sustainable development.

This committee is open to delegates of all skills, welcoming 20 delegates to participate in fruitful debate. Position Papers are mandatory for this committee.
